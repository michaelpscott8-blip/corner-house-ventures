The Opportunity

Why Ireland,
Why Now.

A €9.8 billion foodservice market growing at pace, with the youngest population in the EU and structural tailwinds that favour premium, brand-led concepts.

  • 01
    Europe’s youngest consumers. Ireland’s median age is 39.4 — five years below the EU average. 43% of the population lives in or around Dublin, one of Europe’s most educated and affluent urban centres.
  • 02
    Strong & accelerating spend. Restaurant spending rose 4.4% in 2024, pub and bar spend was up 7.0%, and café and coffee shop revenue grew 5.8%. Ireland’s away-from-home market is projected to reach €15.4 billion by 2030.
  • 03
    Tourism tailwind. 6.4 million international visitors spent €5.5 billion in Ireland in 2025. Dublin consistently ranks as a top European city-break destination, delivering footfall that global brands rely on.
  • 04
    Underpenetrated & policy-supported. Global premium hospitality brands remain underrepresented relative to comparable European cities. A reduced 9% hospitality VAT rate, introduced in Budget 2026, further improves operator economics.

Brand Partners

For hospitality brands
and franchisors.

The best brands in the world — from specialist coffee to experiential dining — are selective about who carries their name into new markets. Groups like Ennismore and AmRest have proven the multi-brand operating model across Europe, scaling concepts from Mama Shelter to Starbucks without losing what makes each one special. Corner House Ventures applies the same discipline to Ireland: a single, dedicated partner who protects the standard.

  • Market analysis, site selection, and lease negotiation across Dublin and key regional cities.
  • On-the-ground launch operations: fitout management, local team build, and supply chain.
  • Full protection of brand standards across design, service, and guest experience.
  • Fewer, better locations — premium positioning over fast footprint expansion.
“The brands that will define the next decade of Irish hospitality haven’t arrived yet. We exist to make sure they land right.”

Capital Partners

For investors
and capital partners.

Premium hospitality is attracting serious capital globally — Dishoom was valued at £300 million after its L Catterton investment, and Blue Bottle changed hands for close to $400 million in early 2026. Ireland offers investors access to the same calibre of brands, in a high-growth market, at an earlier stage.

  • Franchise and licence structures with proven unit economics and limited capital intensity.
  • Exposure to high-loyalty, design-led concepts with strong repeat spend.
  • Technology-driven operations — streamlined back office, real-time reporting, and data-led decision making to maximise unit profitability.
  • Transparent reporting, co-investment alignment, and clear exit pathways.
  • Multi-unit potential in a market growing at a 3.9% CAGR through 2030.
