Option A — Nested Squares

Architectural and precise. Three concentric squares represent the layers of the business: the outer market, the brand partnership, and the core — the venue itself. Nods to floor plans and site mapping. Feels like White Rabbit Projects meets Aesop — geometric, confident, minimal. Works beautifully at any size from a favicon to signage.

Option B — Cornerstone

The "corner" of Corner House, abstracted. An L-shaped bracket references the literal corner of a building — the cornerstone — while the sage dot marks the point of origin, the heart of the venture. Feels open and expansive, suggesting growth from a fixed foundation. The open form has a % Arabica / WatchHouse sensibility — simple, symbolic, architectural.

Option C — CH Monogram

Classic and refined. A serif CH monogram in a thin circle — the most traditional of the three, evoking private members' clubs (Soho House) and luxury hospitality brands. The mixed-case "Corner House" with spaced sans-serif "VENTURES" creates a hierarchy between the hospitality identity and the corporate function. Works particularly well embossed, on stationery, or as a wax seal.
